Who We Are

About the School

★

Swing Society was born from a simple, irresistible obsession: the raw, electric joy of Lindy Hop as it was danced in the 1930s and 40s at Harlem's legendary Savoy Ballroom. We watched the old footage — Frankie Manning flying through the air, Norma Miller tearing up the floor, the whole community of dancers improvising together — and we knew we had to bring that spirit to Bulgaria.

Founded by dancers who travelled to international swing events, learned from master instructors, and fell hopelessly in love with the vernacular jazz tradition, Swing Society opened its doors in Sofia and quickly spread south to Stara Zagora. Our philosophy is simple: swing dancing is a living tradition, not a performance art. We teach it the way it was always meant to be danced — socially, joyfully, and with deep respect for its African-American roots and the musicians who made it all possible.

Today our community spans hundreds of dancers across Bulgaria. Whether you step onto the floor for the first time or have been dancing for years, you'll find a home here. We offer weekly group classes, drop-in sessions, social dances, and special events — all held in venues with good floors, good music, and even better company.

What We Teach

Our Dances

★
Lindy Hop

The Original Swing Dance · 1920s Harlem

Lindy Hop is the mother of all swing dances — an Afro-American vernacular art form born on the dance floors of Harlem during the Jazz Age. It blends African rhythms, European partner dance traditions, and the spontaneous genius of improvisation.

We teach it with attention to its roots: musicality, connection, playfulness, and the deep groove that comes from truly listening to jazz. From your first swing-out to airsteps, we'll take you there step by step.

Beginner Intermediate Advanced
Jazz

Vernacular Solo Jazz · Movement & Expression

Vernacular Jazz is the solo side of the swing world — a rich vocabulary of steps born in Harlem's ballrooms and juke joints. The Charleston, the Shim Sham, the Big Apple: these dances are the living language of jazz music expressed through the body.

Solo jazz classes sharpen your musicality, timing, and personal style. Whether you're a Lindy Hopper looking to deepen your groove or a complete beginner, jazz movement will transform the way you feel the music.

Beginner Intermediate Solo
Tap

Rhythm & Percussion · Your Feet as Instruments

Tap is the art of making music with your feet. Rooted in African percussive traditions and the blues, tap dancers like Bill "Bojangles" Robinson and the Nicholas Brothers turned the stage into a drum kit and made history.

Our classes focus on the improvisational, jazz-driven approach — not Broadway choreography, but the intimate, conversational tap that truly swings. You'll develop timing, musicality, and a vocabulary of rhythm that transforms the way you hear music.
